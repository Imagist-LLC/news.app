Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 2024

The Short Version: news.app doesn't track you. We don't collect personal data. We don't use analytics. We don't have ads. Your reading habits are yours alone.

What We Don't Collect

news.app is designed with privacy as a core principle. We do not collect:

  • Personal information (name, email, location, etc.)
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs
  • Reading history or behavior
  • Analytics or usage data
  • Crash reports with personal information

What Stays On Your Device

All app data remains locally on your device:

  • Bookmarked articles - Stored only in your device's local storage
  • Reading preferences - Never leave your device
  • News mode settings - Kept privately on your device

Third-Party Services

News Content

news.app may fetch articles from public news APIs when in "Real News" mode. These requests:

  • Are made directly from your device
  • Don't include any personal identifiers
  • Are subject to the news provider's own privacy policy

Content Credentials (C2PA)

When verifying content authenticity, the verification happens entirely on your device. No data about what you're reading is sent anywhere.

Data Storage

All data is stored using iOS's local storage mechanisms:

  • UserDefaults for simple preferences
  • Local database for bookmarks
  • No cloud sync or external servers

Children's Privacy

news.app does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13, because we don't collect information from anyone.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever change this privacy policy, we'll update the date at the top. But honestly, we plan to keep it simple: we don't track you.

Contact

Questions about this privacy policy? Reach out through the App Store support link.

Remember: Your privacy isn't a feature, it's a right. news.app respects that.
